        Convenience
        Card image on hover (panel hidden) — when the side panel is collapsed, how to trigger the floating card preview at the pointer. Ctrl + hover is the default (hold Ctrl and move over a card); Always shows the image as soon as you hover any card — no modifier key needed. A local choice.
        Played-cards tab order — Newest first lists plays in strict chronological order, newest at the top (stable, cards never jump). Action flow groups by action status: action-type cards lead, their modifiers & reactions follow, resolved cards trail — cards move within the list as an action resolves, which naturally reflects the live action structure. A local choice.
        Action animations — when a card is played, locked, or unlocked, an enlarged clone pulses centre-screen. Local choice; everyone else still sees animations per their own setting. Mine hidden skips your own (handy when you know what you played); All hidden shows none for you (follow the log instead).
        Animation length — scale how long the action animations last. 100% is the default; lower is snappier, higher is more dramatic. Local only. 100%
        Draw from library / crypt — whether a single click or a double-click draws a card from the library pile or moves a crypt card to uncontrolled. Double-click is the default (less prone to accidental draws); Single click matches the classic feel. Ctrl+click to undo a draw is always single click regardless. Local choice.
        Dock while dragging a card — whether the bottom dock stays open until the dragged card leaves it (default), or closes immediately as the drag starts. Applies to pile drags in every view and hand drags in Classic; in L2/L3 the open hand fan rides out the whole drag (it is the hand). The ⌃ pin tab on the dock's top edge overrides either mode: a pinned dock never auto-closes. Local choice.
        Touch controls — larger tap targets, always-visible pool buttons and (as they land) touch-specific rows in the menus. Auto follows the device (touchscreen → on); force On/Off to override. Gestures themselves (long-press for the menu, drag, pinch) always follow the finger regardless. Local choice.
        Player-colour outlines on cards — in ready & torpor, each card carries its owner's player colour so you can see at a glance whose it is. Local only. Hide home hides your own cards' colour on your own board (keeps it visible when visiting opponents). Mine hidden hides your own everywhere. All hidden hides every player's. (Selection & target rings are separate — below.)
        Brightness — adjust the overall table brightness. 100% is the default. Local only. 100%
        Sound effects volume — master level for all in-game sounds (card plays, lock/unlock, pass bell, oust, your turn). 100% is the default; drag to 0 to mute all SFX. Local only. 0%
        Sound effects — per-channel

        Fine-tune individual sounds relative to the master volume above. 100% = unchanged; 0% = muted. Local only.

        Shortcuts — mouse (fixed)
        Shortcuts — basic

        Click a key chip, then press the new key. Delete clears a binding, Esc cancels. Reassigning a key steals it from its old action, and shortcuts are paused while a dialog is open.

        Play online

        To join a host, enter their address. Same-network friends can just open http://<server-ip>:8123 in a browser. If the host shared a Cloudflare tunnel link, open that https://…trycloudflare.com address (no port needed) and this field fills in for you.
        How you appear to everyone else at the table.
        Pick one to create a room, or type a friend's room name to join it.
        Optional. Only needed to join if the room was created with one.

        Structured: guided zones with columns and helicopter views. Classic: open table — place cards freely, like Lackey. Create only.
        Not connected.

        One player runs node elysium-server.js — everyone else just opens the address above in a browser. Same network, Tailscale/ZeroTier, or a forwarded port all work. The room lives as long as the server runs; a refresh puts you back in your seat.

        Lobby

        Everyone loads a deck and claims a seat. Roll to settle the seating order, then the host starts — the server shuffles and deals. Your seat order becomes the turn order.

        Claim your seat

        Play offline — hotseat

        Seat each player and give them a deck — one screen, pass-and-play. Take priority from the PRIORITY bar to control whoever's acting; a player's hand stays hidden until you take their seat.
